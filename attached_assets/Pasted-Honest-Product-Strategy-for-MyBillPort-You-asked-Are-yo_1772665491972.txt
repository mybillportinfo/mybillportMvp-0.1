Honest Product Strategy for MyBillPort

You asked:

Are you willing to delete features (like Savings Score and Annual Projection) if they don’t support the core promise? Or are you emotionally attached to them?

Here’s my unfiltered answer:

Yes, I am absolutely willing to delete them – if they don’t directly help users never miss a bill.

But let’s be precise:

· Savings Score and Annual Projection are not reminders. They’re financial wellness metrics.
· They do not help someone pay a bill on time.
· They add cognitive load and make the app feel like a budgeting tool, not a bill reminder.

The screenshot you shared shows exactly this problem:

· Top of the dashboard: Annual Projection, Savings Score, Stable spending, Unpaid/Overdue stats.
· Then a “Needs Attention” card, then a list of bills.
· That’s too many things competing for attention.

The core promise is “Never miss a bill again.”
The primary job of the home screen should be to show what needs attention now, and nothing else.

---

🎯 What Supports the Core Promise?

Must-haves on the main screen:

· Greeting (personal touch)
· Clear status: “You have 1 overdue bill” or “All caught up”
· The next urgent bill (with amount, due date, “Pay Now” button)
· Simple monthly summary (Bills this month / Paid this month) – minimal

Nice-to-haves (secondary):

· On‑time payment streak (gamification, encourages consistency)
· Late fees avoided (positive reinforcement)
· Upcoming bills list (already in your “All” tab)
· Savings Score and Annual Projection – these are not reminders; they belong in an insights panel that users can optionally expand.

---

💡 My Recommendation

1. Move Savings Score and Annual Projection out of the main dashboard.
   Put them in a collapsible “Smart Insights” section (as you already described in the redesign prompt).
   This keeps the main screen clean but still offers depth for users who want it.
2. Keep them, but not front and center.
   If you delete them entirely, you lose a potential retention hook – users might enjoy tracking their score over time. But they should never distract from the primary task: paying bills on time.
3. Test with real users.
   If most users never open the insights panel, consider removing them in a future version.
   If they do, keep them. Data, not emotion, should decide.

---

🧠 Are You Emotionally Attached?

Be honest: You probably are.
You built these features, they look cool, and they make the app feel “smart.”
But if they don’t help users accomplish their goal, they’re just noise.

A great product is defined by what it doesn’t have, not what it has.
The best apps are ruthlessly focused on one job.

---

✅ Final Verdict

Feature Keep on Main Screen? Where to Put It
Overdue/Due soon status ✅ Yes Top banner
Next urgent bill card ✅ Yes Primary focus
Monthly summary ✅ Yes Small text summary
On‑time streak 🤔 Maybe Tiny, bottom of list
Savings Score ❌ No Collapsible insights panel
Annual Projection ❌ No Collapsible insights panel
Spending insights ❌ No Collapsible insights panel

Action: Implement the redesign exactly as your master prompt describes.
Then, after launch, monitor how often users expand the insights panel.
If it’s rarely opened, consider removing those features entirely in a later version.

You’re not deleting value – you’re surfacing the right value at the right time.
